# 1. Welcome to The Broken Flagon

Beneath the tavern lies a darkness older than kingdoms. The Broken Flagon stands as the last warm place before the Dungeon of Ash — a 100-floor descent filled with monsters, forgotten kings, cursed relics, and ancient secrets.

Every adventurer faces the same choice: descend into the ash, claim what treasures you can, and return alive if you are able.

## 1.1 Quick Start

- Choose a class and subclass at The Broken Flagon.
- Explore the tavern hub (Floor 0) and talk to its NPCs before you descend.
- Enter the dungeon through the cellar gate. Floors get harder as you descend.
- Fight, loot, and survive. A named boss waits every 5th floor; five are story milestones.
- Die, and the run ends — but gold in the Bank, the Shared Stash, Arena fame, Tavern Renown, and Achievements all carry forward.

## 1.2 Controls

|-------|--------|
| WASD / Arrow Keys | Move |
| Space | Interact (attack, open chest, talk to NPC) |
| E | Use class ability |

# 2. Classes & Subclasses

Every run begins by choosing one of four classes. Each class branches into three subclasses, for a total of 12 distinct playstyles.

## 2.1 Warrior

*High HP and defense. A frontline fighter who can absorb punishment and keep swinging.*

**Base stats: 120 HP · 12 ATK · 8 DEF · No mana**

### Berserker — "Fury without restraint"
*110 HP · 15 ATK · 4 DEF · Special: Rage meter (0–100)*
- Attack power grows as HP drops
- Bonus damage stacks per consecutive kill
- Ignores death once per floor at 1 HP

### Knight — "Discipline forged into iron"
*140 HP · 9 ATK · 12 DEF · Special: Passive block charge*
- Block charge restores on movement
- Reduced damage from adjacent enemies
- Bonus DEF scales with floor depth

### Gladiator — "The crowd demands blood"
*120 HP · 11 ATK · 7 DEF · Special: Riposte counter*
- Counter-attack on enemy misses
- Gold find bonus scales with floor depth
- Stun bonus damage
- Unlocks the Arena from level 1

## 2.2 Rogue

*High crit chance and speed. A skirmisher who strikes hard and disappears.*

**Base stats: 90 HP · 11 ATK · 4 DEF · 30% base crit**

### Assassin — "One strike is all it takes"
*80 HP · 14 ATK · 3 DEF · Special: Shadow Strike (backstab)*
- 40% base crit chance
- Bonus damage on first strike from stealth

### Trickster — "Outwit before you outfight"
*95 HP · 10 ATK · 5 DEF · Special: Set Trap (charges)*
- Traps stun enemies that walk into them
- Bonus crit after dodging

### Shadow — "Darkness is the sharpest blade"
*85 HP · 12 ATK · 4 DEF · Special: Shadow Step (4 mana)*
- Teleports to any visible tile
- Reduced enemy detection range

## 2.3 Mage

*Ranged magic damage. Glass cannon with mana management.*

**Base stats: 70 HP · 9 ATK · 2 DEF · 10 mana**

### Elementalist — "Command the primal forces"
*65 HP · 8 ATK · 1 DEF · Special: Prism Bolt (3 mana)*
- Prism Bolt deals elemental damage, exploiting weaknesses
- Bonus damage on element-weak enemies

### Illusionist — "Nothing is as it seems"
*75 HP · 7 ATK · 3 DEF · Special: Phantom Twin (4 mana)*
- Summons a decoy that draws enemy attention
- Mirror Image evades one attack

### Necromancer — "Death is merely a resource"
*70 HP · 9 ATK · 2 DEF · Special: Raise Dead (5 mana)*
- Raises a skeletal minion from a fallen enemy
- Bone Shield absorbs one hit per floor

## 2.4 Cleric

*Balanced stats. Healing and support abilities.*

**Base stats: 100 HP · 10 ATK · 6 DEF · 8 mana**

### War Domain — "Battle-blessed and unyielding"
*105 HP · 12 ATK · 6 DEF · Special: Smite (3 mana)*
- Smite deals holy damage and stuns briefly

### Light Domain — "Purity radiates through darkness"
*95 HP · 9 ATK · 7 DEF · Special: Searing Light (4 mana)*
- Searing Light can blind enemies (reduces hit chance)
- Crit attacks blind the target

### Twilight Domain — "Guardian of the liminal dark"
*100 HP · 10 ATK · 7 DEF · Special: Moonbeam (3 mana)*
- Moonbeam damages all enemies in a line
- Bonus DEF at night (every other floor)

# 3. Equipment

Equipment drops from enemies, chests, and events. Each piece has a slot, a rarity, a stat bonus, and often a secondary trait.

**Slots:** Weapon · Chest · Helmet · Shield · Boots · Ring · Amulet

Your equipped gear is shown in the Character Sheet (C). Gear auto-equips if it's better than what you're wearing.

## 3.1 Ring & Amulet Effects

Jewelry carries passive traits rather than raw stat bonuses:

| Swift | +movement speed |
| Vitality | +max HP |
| Sharpness | +crit chance |
| Warding | +DEF |
| Lifesteal | Heals % of damage dealt |
| Thorns | Returns damage to attacker |
| Greed | Boosts gold find |

Unidentified items may be cursed. Cursed gear cannot be unequipped while in combat. To remove a curse: use an Identify Scroll before equipping, or visit the **Magic Dealer** (cellar level) for purification.

# 6. Combat & Status Effects

## 6.1 The Basics

Turn-based. Every time you move or act, all enemies take their turn.

- **Attack:** Space (when adjacent to an enemy)
- **Damage:** Your ATK minus the enemy's DEF, minimum 1
- **Critical Hit:** Deals 1.5× damage; chance shown in Character Sheet
- **Lifesteal:** Heals a percentage of each hit dealt

## 6.2 Status Effects

| Status | Effect | Duration |
|--------|--------|----------|
| Poison | Lose HP each turn | 3–6 turns |
| Burn | Lose HP each turn (fire damage) | 2–4 turns |
| Stun | Skip your next turn | 1 turn |
| Weaken | Reduced DEF | 2–3 turns |
| Rage | Enemy gains ATK (from Cultist hex) | 3 turns |
| Blind | Reduced hit chance | 2 turns |

## 6.3 Enemy Telegraphs

Many enemies announce their next action with a floating label above them:

| Label | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| "Draws bow…" | Archer telegraphing a ranged shot next turn |
| "Winds up…" | Brute telegraphing a heavy slam next turn |
| "CHARGING…" | Orc winding up for a massive charge next turn |
| "⚔ PARRY" | Dark Knight in counter stance — striking it hurts you |
| "Raise Dead" | Necromancer about to summon a skeleton |

## 6.4 Element Weaknesses

Some enemies take extra damage from specific elements:

| Enemy | Weakness |
|-------|---------|
| Slime | Fire |
| Skeleton | Lightning |
| Demon | Lightning |

The tavern is Floor 0 — your hub between dungeon runs. Walk to the glowing floor circles and press Space to interact with any NPC.

## 7.2 Tavern Renown

Renown is earned from all your activity — floors descended, bosses killed, Arena wins, daily challenges played. It gates 15 unlocks that progressively make the Flagon feel more alive.

**Earning Renown:**
- +1 per floor descended (run end)
- +8 per milestone boss killed
- +4 per Arena win
- +2 per daily challenge played

**Key Milestones:**

| Renown | Type | Unlock |

| 25 | Ambient | A warrior patron appears by the fire |

## 7.3 The Casino Den

Found in the south courtyard, managed by the Gambler.

**Daily Jackpot** — claims once per day; grows 25g each unclaimed day (base 50g, max 500g). Free spin, no wager required.

**Flagon Dice** — bet on Low (3–10, 2×), High (12–18, 2×), or Triple (all same, 5×).

**Fortune Wheel** — wager gold and spin an 8-segment wheel. Outcomes: BUST (lose all, ~32%), ½× (lose half), 1× (break even), 2×, 3×, 5×, 10×.

# 8. The Overland Map

Beyond the tavern's courtyard door lies the wider realm — a 5×5 overland map you can walk across on foot. Step out of the courtyard and follow the roads.

## 8.1 The Realm

The world is laid out as a grid. The tavern sits at its heart, ringed by forests, roads, and the impassable mountains that wall the realm in.

| | Col 0 | Col 1 | Col 2 | Col 3 | Col 4 |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Row 0** | Mountain | Mountain | Mountain | Mountain | Mountain |
| **Row 1** | Mountain | Forest | Mountain | Forest | Mountain |
| **Row 2** | Forest | **Bravehold** | **The Flagon** | Dungeon Approach | Mountain |
| **Row 3** | Forest | Crossroads | **The Pit** | East Fork | Forest |
| **Row 4** | Forest | Forest | Forest | Forest | Forest |

## 8.2 Named Locations

- **The Broken Flagon** *(centre)* — your home base. The tavern hub, its NPCs, the dungeon entrance, and the courtyard.
- **The City of Bravehold** *(west of the tavern)* — a walled settlement with its own General Store, Temple, Alchemist, and Town Hall.
- **The Pit** *(south of the tavern)* — the arena. Locked until you survive Floor 20. See Section 9.
- **The roads** — the Dungeon Approach, the Crossroads, and the East Fork connect the realm.

## 8.3 Travelling

Walk onto a glowing **zone exit** tile — marked with a directional arrow (↑ ↓ ← →) — to cross into the neighbouring area. The mountains cannot be entered; walk into one and you'll be turned back.

The forests and roads are open ground for now — quiet country between the tavern and the dark. Future updates will fill them with encounters, foragers, and roadside dangers.

# 9. The Pit — Arena

The Arena unlocks when you reach Floor 20 (or immediately for the Gladiator subclass). From the tavern courtyard, take the road **south** to The Pit. If you haven't yet earned your place, a voice will turn you away at the gate.

## 9.1 Arena Seasons

The Pit runs in four seasons, each with its own roster and rewards:

| Season | Fame Range | Identity |
|--------|-----------|---------|
| Bronze | 0–74 | Sparse crowd, starter opponents |
| Silver | 75–199 | Regulars know your name, new challengers |
| Gold | 200–399 | Merchants pay for seats, bards write songs |
| Champion | 400+ | The Pit has no ceiling for you |

## 9.2 Arena Titles

| Fame | Title | Season |
|------|-------|--------|
| 0 | Unknown | Bronze |
| 25 | Challenger | Bronze |
| 75 | Contender | Silver |
| 125 | Gladiator | Silver |
| 200 | Champion | Gold |
| 300 | Warlord | Gold |
| 400 | Legend | Champion |
| 600 | Undying | Champion |

## 9.3 Fighting

Two ways to enter a bout:

**Champion fights** — fixed roster opponents, always available once fame requirement is met.

**Captured creatures** — weaken any dungeon enemy below 30% HP and use a Capture Net (key 6). Bring it to the Arena to fight it for custom rewards.

**Betting** — before each bout, bet gold. If you win, you collect gold × the displayed odds. Betting odds increase with your fame tier.

**Non-ironman rule** — losing an Arena bout doesn't kill your character. You're ejected and lose your bet. Ironman mode is optional.

# 11. The Dungeon of Ash

The dungeon spans 100 floors. Every floor is procedurally generated and gets harder as you go deeper.

## 11.1 Regular Enemies (Floors 1–16)

| Enemy | Floor | Notes |
|-------|-------|-------|
| Goblin | 1+ | Common threat. Low stats. |
| Slime | 1+ | High HP, soft hits. Weak to fire. May poison. |
| Skeleton | 2+ | Standard melee. Weak to lightning. May weaken. |
| Archer | 3+ | Ranged. Telegraphs shot one turn early. |
| Brute | 4+ | Telegraphs a heavy slam one turn early. |
| Bat | 5+ | Erratic flyer, quick double-peck. |
| Spider | 6+ | Fast; stacks poison on every hit. |
| Cultist | 8+ | Buffs adjacent allies instead of attacking. Kill first. |
| Thief | 12+ | Steals gold on hit, then flees. |
| Necromancer | 14+ | Raises skeleton minions. Silence it first. |
| Warden | 16+ | Tank. Reliably weakens on hit. Cannot be stunned. |

## 11.2 Elite Enemies (Floors 20–50+)

| Enemy | Floor | Mechanic |
|-------|-------|---------|
| Imp | 20+ | Burns on every hit. 50% chance to strike twice. |
| Ratman | 22+ | Ranged archer. Retreats when you get adjacent. |
| Ghoul | 25+ | Heals 50% of all damage it deals. Burst it down. |
| Lizardman | 30+ | Regenerates HP each turn. End fights quickly. |
| Orc | 35+ | Telegraphs a massive charge one turn early. |
| Dark Knight | 40+ | Enters parry stance. Striking it during parry damages you. |
| Demon | 50+ | Fire-immune. Burns you on hit. Weak to lightning. |

## 11.3 The Mimic

On Floor 15+, some chest tiles are Mimics in disguise. They appear identical to a Common Chest. Opening them spawns a Mimic enemy that attacks immediately. There is no reliable way to detect a Mimic in advance — this is by design. Trust nothing on the deep floors.

## 11.4 Floor Bosses

Every 5th floor spawns a boss with boosted stats. Four recurring variants cycle as you descend:

- **The Splitter** — divides when struck; kill it quickly.
- **The Necromancer** — raises fallen minions. Kill it before they accumulate.
- **The Iron Sentinel** — phases between armored and vulnerable. Strike the vulnerable window.
- **The Void Wraith** — teleports randomly; unpinnable.

## 11.5 The Five Milestone Bosses

| Floor | Boss | Difficulty |
|-------|------|-----------|
| 10 | The Goblin King | 1.2× HP · 1.1× ATK |
| 25 | The Bone Dragon | 1.5× HP · 1.3× ATK |
| 50 | The Lich Lord | 1.8× HP · 1.4× ATK |
| 75 | The Demon Prince | 2.0× HP · 1.6× ATK |
| 100 | The Fallen God | 2.5× HP · 2.0× ATK |

Defeating a milestone boss permanently unlocks its Achievement, recharges any spent Phoenix Feather relic, and sends a legendary guest NPC to the tavern.

## 10.6 Seed Codes

Every run is generated from a 7-character seed code. The same code always produces the exact same dungeon.

- **Share your run:** open the Character Sheet (C) and copy your seed code.
- **Play a specific seed:** type a code in the Seed field on the character creation screen.

# 12. Character Progression

## 11.1 Experience & Leveling

Defeating enemies grants XP. XP needed to level: 25 × current level.

On level-up: full heal, choose a permanent bonus (ATK, HP, DEF, Crit, Lifesteal, or Mana for spellcasters).

## 11.2 The Bestiary

Press **B** to open the Bestiary/Codex. Every enemy you encounter is logged with its lore, stats, and your kill count. Discover all entries for the Archivist achievement.

## 11.3 The Fallen

Every time a character dies, they're added to The Fallen — a persistent graveyard shown on the title screen and in the legend. Your last 8 heroes are remembered by name, class, level, floor reached, and what killed them.

## 11.4 What Carries Between Runs

**Carries forward:**
- Gold deposited in the Bank vault
- Items in the Shared Stash (3 slots)
- All Achievements
- Arena fame and season rank
- Tavern Renown (all 15 milestones)
- Best floor reached
- Bestiary progress
- The Fallen graveyard
- Permanent Tavern Upgrades
- Daily Casino jackpot

**Resets on death:**
- Your level, gear, relics, inventory, and gold on hand

# 13. UI & Visual Enhancements (v2.0)

Version 2.0 is a comprehensive visual and UX overhaul of every major screen in the game.

## 13.1 Character Creation

Each class card now features a living particle canvas with class-specific effects: fire embers for Warrior, shadow smoke and dagger streaks for Rogue, orbiting arcane particles for Mage, and rising golden motes for Cleric. Gender selection uses glowing symbol buttons below the character portrait. The tavern header rotates context-aware quotes and features a hover-reactive logo with ember particles.

## 13.2 Death & Victory Screen

The run-end screen is now a full-screen overlay with falling ash (death) or rising gold confetti (victory), staggered reveal animations, character portrait, cause-of-death banner, and an 8-stat grid. Red theming for death, gold for victory.

## 13.3 Level-Up Screen

Each stat choice now shows a before/after comparison badge. Cards cascade in with staggered delays. Selecting a choice flashes it gold while dimming the others.

## 13.4 Boss Encounter Reveal

Milestone bosses trigger a cinematic overlay. The boss glyph zooms in, the name spells out letter by letter, and the announce text fades in. Click anywhere to skip.

## 13.5 NPC Panel Differentiation

Every tavern vendor panel has its own accent color and atmospheric gradient: Innkeeper amber, Blacksmith forge orange, Gambler green, Bard purple, Magic Dealer arcane blue.

## 13.6 Item Rarity Visual System

Legendary and Mythic items have a diagonal shine sweep animation. Epic+ items show inner corner accents. Tooltip names display in rarity color. Hover glow scales with tier.

## 13.7 Combat Log

Per-category entrance animations: damage-in red flash, crit gold pop, poison green, fire orange, frost blue, kill red pulse, milestone gold glow. Only the newest entry animates.

## 13.8 Achievement Toasts

Animated toast slides in from the top-right on achievement unlock. 4s auto-dismiss. Multiple toasts stack.

## 13.9 Run History & Leaderboard

"Past Runs" shows your last 5 completed runs. The Global Leaderboard is accessible from the tavern and death screen.

## 13.10 Mobile & Touch Controls

Larger d-pad buttons, swipe gesture support, and single-column responsive layout for narrow screens.

## 1.10.0

- **Arena Seasons:** The Pit now runs in four structured seasons — Bronze, Silver, Gold, and Champion — each with its own opponent roster, visual identity, and season-promotion announcement. 13 total opponents across all seasons (was 6).
- **Dungeon Events:** Each run now has a ~60% chance of a named event that colours the whole descent: Goblin Migration, Necromancer Activity, Spider Infestation, Cultist Uprising, Ash Storm, and Blessed Week. Active event shown in the Run Snapshot panel.
- **Elite/Deep-Floor Enemies:** Seven new enemy types for floors 20–50+: Imp (double-strike fire), Ratman (retreating archer), Ghoul (lifesteal), Lizardman (regenerating), Orc (telegraphed charge), Dark Knight (parry counter), Demon (fire-immune). Each with sprite art, bestiary lore, intent prediction, and distinct AI.
- **The Mimic:** A chest tile that ambushes the player on floor 15+. Appears identical to a Common Chest. Opening it spawns a Mimic enemy and triggers an event card.
- **Tavern Renown:** 15 milestones (10 ambient, 5 structural) earned from all play sources. Ambient unlocks add patrons, dialogue, and visual details; structural unlocks add merchant slots, harder bounties, extra brew, expanded bank cap, and a champion perk.
- **Shop Physical Identity:** Each NPC area now has environmental dressing — forge glow animation at the Blacksmith, vault door at the Bank, crate shelves at the Merchant, practice dummy at the Trainer, bar counter and kegs at the Innkeeper.
- **Storybook Modal:** "Read the Full Legend" replaced with a full-screen parchment overlay with SVG chapter illustrations, animated unfurl, and a revised ending hook. Button renamed "Descend into the Ash."
- **The Fallen:** Persistent graveyard of last 8 dead characters shown on the title screen and inside the storybook. Records name, class, level, floor, and cause of death.
- **Ash Particles:** Drifting ambient particles on the title screen with ember flicker. Stops when you begin a run.
- **Legend Panel Icons:** All item and interactable entries now use inline SVG icons instead of text glyphs. Tavern Hub entries use real NPC sprite art. Rarity tiers use distinct star icons.
- **First-Session Onboarding:** New players see a focused welcome with a tavern tour hint and a post-death persistence hint (first 3 deaths only). Returning players see a shorter greeting.
- **Electron Packaging:** Desktop wrapper for Windows, macOS, and Linux with Steam-ready build configuration.
