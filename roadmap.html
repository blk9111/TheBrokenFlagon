Roadmap

Where the cellar goes from here — a plan that descends in order of certainty.

Next · the floor below

Finish what's started

Small, high-certainty work that closes gaps the audit found — systems whose logic exists but whose UI or wiring hasn't caught up.

Build the Lotería UI

Medium

The engine is done and tested; it needs a front end. Render the tabla(s), animate the caller, and wire click-to-mark. Either set the engine's markableUntil window during the build, or extend the engine to own the timing so the skill rule is consistent.

Why now: a finished engine with no UI is a feature players can't reach.

Wire the missing telemetry

Small

Populate bossesKilled, potionsUsed, and damage-efficiency so balance analytics stop reading zero. Cheap to fix, unblocks every future tuning decision.

Why now: the next balance pass is blind without it.

Unit-test the new logic

Small

Add assertions for the Lotería any-line win and payout floor, and for the arena rivals data contract now that it's wired. Both are pure logic — ideal, fast tests.

Why now: lock in this session's fixes before they can regress.

Soon · deeper down

Strengthen the foundations

Planned work that pays off over time — balance, maintainability, and proving the 100-floor climb is real.

Resolve the floor-100 completability question

Medium

Run completion mode to a definitive answer: does a legitimate clear happen, and if not, where's the wall? Turn the result into a balance change or an assertion-backed proof, not just a bot anecdote.

Why: "a human can finish" should be a fact, not a hope.

Investigate the lagging specialization

Small

Focused batches on Twilight Domain (_bot.toggleCls) to isolate whether it's under-tuned, mis-built, or mis-played by the bot — then tune or fix accordingly.

Why: every build should feel viable at the floors it's meant for.

Tame the CSS

Medium

Gradually retire the ~197 !important flags by fixing specificity, and consolidate the color hexes behind the existing variables. Opens the door to a high-contrast or alternate theme later.

Why: today it works; the cost is future flexibility.

Someday · the bottom

Grow the world

Aspirational directions — bigger bets that depend on the foundations above being solid first. Not commitments; possibilities worth holding onto.

Split the renderer

Large

When render.js next needs real work, break it along natural seams — world-draw, HUD, effects — and bring the pure-logic/tested discipline of the newer modules to the oldest, largest one.

Why someday: high value, high risk — do it when there's a reason to be in there anyway.

More content depth

Large

New enemy archetypes for the deep floors, more Treasury upgrade branches, additional Pit seasons or champion rivalries, and Lotería card art to replace the unicode stand-ins.

Why someday: content scales the game once the systems are locked.

Accessibility & polish pass

Medium

Screen-reader labels on icon buttons, dialog roles on overlays, a colorblind-friendly rarity palette, and the high-contrast theme the CSS cleanup unlocks.

Why someday: broadens who can play, once the surface is stable.

How to read this Horizons are ordered by certainty, not just time. "Next" items are nearly committed and low-risk; "Soon" is planned but flexible; "Someday" is a holding pen for good ideas that need the foundations finished first. Effort tags (Small / Medium / Large) are honest estimates for a solo developer, not promises of dates.
